The 2025 season is in the books, and with the Super Bowl behind us, the NFL world turns its attention to Indianapolis and the 2026 NFL Draft. This mock draft projects all 32 first-round picks based on team needs, prospect fit, and the current draft landscape. Here’s how the first round could shake out.

---

## 1. Las Vegas Raiders — Fernando Mendoza, QB, Indiana

**6'5" | 225 lbs**

Mendoza capped an incredible career with a Heisman Trophy and a national championship at Indiana. He brings elite accuracy, sharp decision-making, and the poise to lead an NFL offense from day one. The Raiders land their franchise quarterback and reset the direction of the organization.

## 2. New York Jets — Arvell Reese Jr., LB, Ohio State

**6'4" | 243 lbs**

Reese is a top-five talent and an All-American who can play linebacker or work off the edge. His versatility and athleticism make him a cornerstone piece for a Jets defense looking to build around blue-chip prospects. He can drop into coverage, blitz, or shut down the run.

## 3. Arizona Cardinals — Francis Mauigoa, OT, Miami

**6'6" | 315 lbs**

Mauigoa is the top tackle in this class and a Consensus All-American. He offers dominant pass protection and reliable run blocking, giving Arizona a long-term answer at left tackle and immediate stability along the offensive line.

## 4. Tennessee Titans — Rueben Bain Jr., DL, Miami

**6'3" | 275 lbs**

Bain was one of the nation’s most dominant pass rushers at Miami. He has prototype edge size, power, and quickness, and he’s shown he can consistently pressure the quarterback. The Titans add a cornerstone defender to build around.

## 5. New York Giants — Caleb Downs, S, Ohio State

**6'0" | 205 lbs**

Downs is an elite safety with range, instincts, and ball skills. His ability to cover ground, read quarterbacks, and make plays on the ball makes him a difference-maker in any secondary. The Giants get a defensive leader for years to come.

## 6. Cleveland Browns — Spencer Fano, OL, Utah

**6'5" | 305 lbs**

Fano is a versatile offensive lineman who can line up at tackle or guard. He excels in pass protection and run blocking, and his flexibility gives Cleveland options as they reshape their front. A steady, high-floor pick.

## 7. Washington Commanders — David Bailey, DL, Texas Tech

**6'3" | 250 lbs**

Bailey was among the most productive pass rushers in the country. His explosive first step and relentless motor show up every snap. Washington adds a disruptive presence who can change games from the edge or inside.

## 8. New Orleans Saints — Jordyn Tyson, WR, Arizona State

**6'2" | 200 lbs**

Tyson brings size, athleticism, and refined route-running. He’s a weapon in contested-catch situations and can stretch the field. New Orleans gets a dynamic target to pair with their passers for the next era.

## 9. Kansas City Chiefs — Jeremiyah Love, RB, Notre Dame

**6'0" | 214 lbs**

Love is an elite three-down back with vision, power, and receiving ability. He can handle every down, pass protect, and produce in the screen game. The Chiefs add a back who fits their scheme and can carry the load.

## 10. Cincinnati Bengals — Sonny Styles, LB, Ohio State

**6'4" | 243 lbs**

Styles is a freakishly athletic linebacker with sideline-to-sideline speed, strong tackling, and coverage skills. He can match up with backs and tight ends and fly to the ball against the run. Cincinnati strengthens the heart of their defense.

## 11. Miami Dolphins — Jermod McCoy, CB, Tennessee

**6'0" | 193 lbs**

McCoy is the consensus top cornerback in the class and a First-Team All-SEC performer. His ball skills, coverage versatility, and instincts translate to immediate impact. Miami lands a lockdown corner for a division that stresses the secondary.

## 12. Dallas Cowboys — Mansoor Delane, CB, LSU

**6'1" | 185 lbs**

Delane can play nickel, outside, or safety. His instincts and tackling complement his coverage ability. Dallas gets a versatile defensive back who can adapt to different schemes and matchups.

## 13. Los Angeles Rams — Makai Lemon, WR, USC

**5'11" | 195 lbs**

Lemon, the Biletnikoff Award winner, is an elite route runner and slot weapon. He has reliable hands and the ability to create separation. The Rams add a polished receiver who fits their offensive system.

## 14. Baltimore Ravens — Olaivavega Ioane, OL, Penn State

**6'4" | 328 lbs**

Ioane is a dominant interior lineman and First-Team All-Big Ten selection. He’s a violent run blocker and a reliable pass protector. Baltimore reinforces the trenches with a mauler who fits their identity.

## 15. Tampa Bay Buccaneers — Cashius Howell, DL, Texas A&M

**6'2" | 248 lbs**

Howell, the SEC Defensive Player of the Year, posted 11.5 sacks and earned unanimous All-American honors. He’s an elite speed rusher who can bend the edge and close on quarterbacks. Tampa Bay gets a game-changer on the defensive line.

## 16. New York Jets — Carnell Tate, WR, Ohio State

**6'3" | 195 lbs**

Tate is a contested-catch specialist who can win at every level. He continues Ohio State’s tradition of producing first-round receivers. The Jets add a reliable target and red-zone weapon.

## 17. Detroit Lions — Peter Woods, DL, Clemson

**6'3" | 310 lbs**

Woods is a five-star interior disruptor with a quick first step and the ability to play across the defensive line. His versatility and motor make him a perfect fit for Detroit’s attacking front.

## 18. Minnesota Vikings — Emmanuel McNeil-Warren, S, Toledo

**6'3" | 209 lbs**

McNeil-Warren is a rising star with elite length, instincts, and zone coverage skills. A Senior Bowl invitee, he’s shown he can compete with Power 5 talent. Minnesota finds a rangy, smart safety for the back end.

## 19. Carolina Panthers — CJ Allen, LB, Georgia

**6'1" | 235 lbs**

Allen is considered the best linebacker in the class. His run defense and instincts earned him All-American honors, and his style draws Roquan Smith comparisons. Carolina gets a tone-setter in the middle of the defense.

## 20. Dallas Cowboys — Kayden McDonald, DL, Ohio State

**6'3" | 326 lbs**

McDonald is a unanimous All-American and the nation’s top run defender. He anchors an elite Ohio State defense and projects as a disruptive interior force. Dallas shores up their defensive front with a standout tackle.

## 21. Pittsburgh Steelers — Ty Simpson, QB, Alabama

**6'2" | 208 lbs**

Simpson broke out as a pocket passer with 28 touchdowns and five interceptions, leading Alabama to an SEC title. His mechanics are NFL-ready and his arm talent is evident. Pittsburgh lands a quarterback who can develop into a long-term starter.

## 22. Los Angeles Chargers — Keldric Faulk, DL, Auburn

**6'6" | 285 lbs**

Faulk brings length, run defense, and a strong bull rush off the edge. He’s a first-round projection with the frame to hold up against the run and the athleticism to rush the passer. The Chargers add a versatile defensive lineman.

## 23. Philadelphia Eagles — Monroe Freeling, OL, Georgia

**6'7" | 315 lbs**

Freeling is a smooth, rangy tackle who can play either side. He brings four-star pedigree and strong run-game ability. Philadelphia invests in the offensive line with a prospect who can anchor the unit for years.

## 24. Cleveland Browns — KC Concepcion, WR, Texas A&M

**5'11" | 190 lbs**

Concepcion won the Paul Hornung Award as college football’s most versatile player. He’s dynamic with the ball in his hands, can line up in the slot or outside, and is a threat in the return game. Cleveland adds explosiveness to their offense.

## 25. Chicago Bears — T.J. Parker, DL, Clemson

**6'3" | 260 lbs**

Parker is an elite edge rusher with an explosive first step. He tallied six sacks and 41 pressures in 2025. Chicago gets an impact pass rusher to build their front around.

## 26. Buffalo Bills — Denzel Boston, WR, Washington

**6'4" | 210 lbs**

Boston has elite hands and a 76.9% contested-catch rate. He scored 11 touchdowns in 2025 and can win at every level. Buffalo adds a reliable, physical target for their passing attack.

## 27. San Francisco 49ers — Emmanuel Pregnon, OL, Oregon

**6'5" | 318 lbs**

Pregnon is an elite run blocker who allowed zero sacks. He’s the top guard prospect in the class and a First-Team All-Pac-12 selection. San Francisco reinforces their offensive line with a mauler who fits their scheme.

## 28. Houston Texans — Kadyn Proctor, OL, Alabama

**6'7" | 366 lbs**

Proctor has the potential to be the No. 1 tackle in the class. A five-star recruit and Freshman All-American, he was a Lombardi Award finalist. Houston invests in their future at left tackle.

## 29. Los Angeles Rams — Avieon Terrell, CB, Clemson

**5'11" | 180 lbs**

Terrell, A.J. Terrell’s brother, is a lockdown corner with 12 pass breakups and two interceptions in 2024. The Rams add another weapon to their secondary with their second first-round pick.

## 30. Denver Broncos — Kenyon Sadiq, TE, Oregon

**6'3" | 245 lbs**

Sadiq is a top-three tight end in the class with 46 catches and eight touchdowns. He’s a willing blocker and a red-zone threat. Denver gets a well-rounded tight end who can contribute immediately.

## 31. New England Patriots — Caleb Lomu, OL, Utah

**6'6" | 308 lbs**

Lomu was ESPN’s No. 1 pure tackle and allowed zero sacks. A First-Team All-Big 12 selection, he’s a polished pass protector. New England addresses the offensive line with a high-floor prospect.

## 32. Seattle Seahawks — Lee Hunter, DL, Texas Tech

**6'4" | 330 lbs**

Hunter is a true nose tackle and First-Team All-Big 12 run-stuffer. His size and power allow him to anchor against double teams. Seattle closes the first round with a difference-maker in the middle of their defense.
